Asset 03 — Cold Outreach
Cold Email Sequence (3 emails)

Target: US/UK independent restaurant owners, food delivery startups, ghost kitchen operators. Find them on Google Maps, Yelp, or LinkedIn. Send in order, 3–4 days apart.

How to Find Targets
  • 🔍
    Google Maps: Search "healthy restaurant [US city]" → visit their website → check if it has basic SEO issues (no meta description, no schema, slow mobile load). If yes — they're a lead.
  • 🔍
    LinkedIn: Search "restaurant owner" + "food delivery" + location filter (US, UK, Australia). Filter by 2nd connections.
  • 🔍
    Find email: Use Hunter.io (free tier) or check their website's contact page. Most small restaurant owners list their email publicly.
Email 1 of 3 — Day 1
Subject Line
Quick SEO thing I noticed about [Restaurant Name]
Body
Hi [First Name], I was looking up [Restaurant Name] on Google while researching food businesses in [City] — and noticed something that's probably costing you search visibility. Your website doesn't have structured data (schema markup) set up. This is the code that tells Google you're a restaurant — your hours, menu, location, and cuisine type. Without it, Google is guessing. With it, you're eligible for rich results in search (star ratings, hours, menu previews). It's a 2–3 hour fix. And for a local restaurant, it can move you up in both regular search and Google Maps results. I specialise in SEO for food businesses — I run a cloud kitchen myself, so I understand the space from the inside. Would it be useful if I sent you a 5-minute Loom video showing exactly what's missing on your site and how to fix it? No cost, no obligation. Altaf [Title: SEO for Food Brands] [Email / LinkedIn / Upwork URL]

Asset 05 — Launch Roadmap
Your First 30 Days

Zero to first international client. Realistic, sequential, no fluff.

Week 1 — Build
Set up your presence
Create Upwork profile using the copy above. Set up LinkedIn with the new headline + About. Write the NOSH7 case study as a portfolio piece. Install Loom (free) — you'll use it to record quick audits. Create a simple email signature with your Upwork link.
Week 2 — Launch
First applications + first outreach
Apply to 5 Upwork jobs/day. Search "restaurant SEO," "local SEO food," "Google Business Profile." Also publish the LinkedIn launch post. Send the first 30 cold emails (10/day, 3 days). Focus on US cities with strong independent restaurant scenes: Austin, Portland, Nashville, Denver.
Week 3 — Warm Up
Follow up + first calls
Send Email 2 to non-responders. Accept any Upwork interview immediately — response speed matters for ranking. Offer a free 15-minute Loom audit to any prospect who replies. The Loom is your sales tool — it shows skill without a live call across time zones.
Week 4 — Close
Convert to first paid project
Send Email 3 to all cold leads. For any warm Upwork leads, offer a small fixed-price starter project ($150–$250 audit) to remove risk for the client. One paid project + one 5-star review changes everything. After first review, raise your rate and tighten your niche pitch.
